# Final Deliverable — Integrated Analytics Strategy & Executive Recommendation

## Theme: From Insight to Action

You are no longer analysts presenting outputs.
You are strategic advisors making a decision recommendation backed by analytics.

---

## Objective

Synthesize your full semester work into a **cohesive, executive-ready narrative** that:

- Connects **retrospective insights → predictive models → real-time strategy → data expansion**
- Demonstrates **judgment, not just analysis**
- Ends with a **clear, defensible recommendation**

This is your opportunity to show you understand **how analytics actually creates value in organizations**.

---

## Deliverables

### 1. Final Presentation (Group)

**Length:** 12–15 minutes
**Format:** Slides (PowerPoint / Google Slides)

Your presentation should tell a **single, cohesive story**, not four separate assignments stitched together.

---

### Required Structure

#### 1. The Business Problem (Set the Stakes)

- What decision is being made?
- Why does it matter?
- What would happen if nothing changed?

---

#### 2. Retrospective Insights (What Happened?)

- Key findings from Deliverable 1
- Patterns, trends, or anomalies that matter
- What these insights suggest about the business

👉 Focus on **interpretation, not re-showing charts**

---

#### 3. Predictive Insights (What Might Happen?)

- Summary of your model and approach (brief)
- Key predictions and what they imply
- Uncertainty, assumptions, and limitations

👉 The goal is not technical depth—it's **decision relevance**

---

#### 4. Real-Time Strategy (What Should We Monitor Now?)

- What would a real-time system track?
- Mockups or conceptual dashboards
- How this changes decision speed or quality

👉 Show how the organization becomes **more responsive**

---

#### 5. Expanding the Data Imagination (What Are We Missing?)

- What additional data could improve decisions?
- Why wasn't it used?
- Tradeoffs: cost, complexity, risk

👉 Demonstrate **restraint and judgment**, not "more data is always better"

---

#### 6. Final Recommendation (What Should Be Done?)

This is the most important section.

- Clear, specific recommendation
- What action should leadership take?
- Why this is the best path forward

---

#### 7. Risk & Tradeoffs

- What could go wrong?
- What assumptions matter most?
- What would change your recommendation?

---

#### 8. Implementation View (Make It Real)

- What would it take to implement this?
- Timeline (high-level)
- Organizational or data constraints

---

## 2. Executive Summary (Individual Writing Component)

Each group submits a **2-page executive summary** (concise, polished, professional).

### Audience:

Senior leadership (non-technical, time-constrained)

---

### Required Structure

#### Paragraph 1: The Decision

- What decision needs to be made?
- Why it matters now

---

#### Paragraph 2: What We Learned

- Key retrospective insight
- Key predictive takeaway

---

#### Paragraph 3: What Changes

- How real-time analytics improves decision-making
- What additional data could (or could not) add

---

#### Paragraph 4: Recommendation

- Clear, direct recommendation
- No hedging

---

#### Paragraph 5: Risks & Considerations

- Key uncertainties
- What leadership should watch

---

### Writing Expectations

- No technical jargon
- No charts
- No filler
- Every sentence should drive toward a decision

---

## Evaluation Criteria

### 1. Cohesion of the Analytic Story (25%)

- Does the work feel integrated or fragmented?
- Are insights connected logically?

---

### 2. Quality of Recommendation (25%)

- Is it clear, actionable, and defensible?
- Does it reflect real judgment?

---

### 3. Use of Analytics (20%)

- Are insights used appropriately (not over/under-relied on)?
- Are limitations understood?

---

### 4. Strategic Thinking (15%)

- Evidence of tradeoff awareness
- Understanding of business implications

---

### 5. Communication & Clarity (15%)

- Executive-level storytelling
- Clear, concise, compelling

---

## What Strong Projects Will Do

- Tell a **tight, compelling story**
- Show **restraint** (not everything needs to be included)
- Make a **clear recommendation early and support it**
- Treat analytics as **input into judgment—not the answer itself**
